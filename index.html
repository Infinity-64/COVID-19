COVID-19

COVID-19 is a virus that is part of a group of viruses called coronavirus.


COVID-19 is often remembered as coronavirus, when it's actually COVID-19.


COVID-19 stands for COronaVIrus Disease 2019.


One theory is that it all started at a seafood market in Wuhan, China and eventually spread to more and more places, eventually leading to most countries in the world.


How to protect yourself
  • It is thought to spread when someone that is sick with COVID-19 coughs or you get less than 6 feet away
  • Wash your hands often for at least 20 seconds after using the restroom, coming home, or before eating something
  • Stay as far away as people that are sick with COVID-19 and in the public, stay away from any people, even if it's your best friend

How to protect others if you're sick
  • Stay home
  • Cover all your coughs and sneezes
  • Wear a surgical mask to prevent germs from sprerading
  • Stay away from people
  • Stay in a different room at home

The following types of people are more vulnerable to COVID-19:
  • Old adults
  • People that have:
    • Heart Disease
    • Diabetes
    • Lung Disease

Note: young people are less vulnerable and have a lower chance of dying, including kids.
